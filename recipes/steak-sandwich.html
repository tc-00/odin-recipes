Steak Sandwich Recipe

Home

Description

This steak sandwich features a juicy, grilled steak served on a toasted baguette with arugula, cheese, and a spicy, creamy sauce. It's a delicious and satisfying meal perfect for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients

For the caramelised onions

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 large red onions (sliced)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup water (plus more as needed)

For the chipotle mayo

  • 1 cup mayonaise
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 3 chipotle peppers
  • 3 tablespoons adobo sauce

For the sandwiches

  • 1 large baguette
  • 1 1/2 pounds skirt steak
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoon neutral oil
  • 1/2 lb provolone cheese
  • 1-2 cups baby arugula
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (plus more if needed)

Steps

For the caramelised onions

  1. Add onions, water, olive oil, and salt to a large pan and mix together. Turn heat to medium-high and cook covered for 12-15 minutes or until the onions are soft and most of the water has evaporated.
  2. Uncover the pot and turn down the heat to medium. Continue to saute until onions are deeplt caramelised (about 30 minutes more). Keep an eye on the onions. Adjust heat and/or add splash of water to prevent burning

For the chiptole mayo

  1. MIx the Ingredients and give it a taste. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

For the sandwiches

  1. Preheat the oven to 450f and set the rack to the middle level.
  2. Heat a large cast iron or stainless steel pan to medium-high heat.
  3. Cut the skirt steak into half or thirds so that it can fit into the pan. Pat the steak dry with paper towels and season liberally with salt and pepper on both sides.
  4. Add the oil to the pan and once smoking, add the steak. Sear for 3 minutes per side or until well browned. Place the cooked steak on a cutting board and tent with foil for at least 5 minutes. After 5 minutes cut the steak into 4-inch pieces then slice against the grain into 1/4-inch thick slices.
  5. Cut the baguette down the middle lengthwise and seperate the two sides. Place the bottom side of the baguette onto a foil lined baking sheet. Spread a layer of the chipotle mayo then layer the steak. Evenly spread the onions on top and then layer the provolone cheese. Place the top side of the baguette onto the baking sheet and spread a thin layer of olive oil so that it will toast.
  6. Bake until the cheese melts (about 3-5 minutes). Watch carefully!
  7. Spread the chipotle mayo on the top side of baguette. Distribute the optional arugula on top and then close the sandwich. Slice into 4 large or sandwiches. Enjoy!
