Smash Burgers Recipe

Home

Description

Smash burgers are a type of hamburger that is made by smashing a ball of ground beef onto a hot griddle or skillet, creating a crispy, caramelized crust. They are typically served on a soft bun with various toppings.

Ingredients

  • 4 brioche burger buns
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 500g/1lb ground beef (15-20% fat)
  • 4 slices cheddar cheese
  • 1 red onion
  • iceberg lettuce
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 1 gherkin
  • ketchup, mayonaise, mustard, or sauce of your preference

Steps

  1. Slice the brioche buns in half, then toast or grill and set aside. Heat the oven to 100C/80C fan/gas 1/4
  2. Divide mince into 4 piles and season with salt. Heat 1/4 olive oil in heavy frying pan over high heat and put one of the piles in the pan. Cover with baking paper and use a smaller pan to press the beef patty as hard as you can. Allow to cook for 2 minutes.
  3. Remove baking paper and flip the patty. Press down with spatula, cover with cheddar slice and cook for 1 minute. Transfer to baking tray and keep warm in oven whilst you cook the other burgers the same way.
  4. Assemble the burger. Add to the bun base: onion, lettuce and tomato, followed by smashed patty, gherkin slices, sauce of your choice, and the bun top.
